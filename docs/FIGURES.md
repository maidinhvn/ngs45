### Figure 1 — Read length ≥150 bp enables short-read 45S assembly
![Figure 1](figures/Figure1_readlength.png)

ngs45 unit identity to the HiFi consensus vs Illumina read length. Green = full
unit recovered; red × = not assembled. Grey arrows link the *same species* from
its old short-read run (failure) to a modern ≥150 bp run (success). Below ~150 bp
the rDNA spacer variants cannot be phased and assembly fragments; ≥150 bp recovers
the unit at 99.75–100 % identity. *Musa* is the exception — it fails even at 309 bp
(intrinsic rDNA heterozygosity; needs HiFi).

### Figure 2 — Recovery rate
![Figure 2](figures/Figure2_success_rate.png)

Full-unit recovery across 10 species / 9 orders: ngs45 4/10 with old (<150 bp)
input → **9/10 with modern (≥150 bp)** input; easy45/HiFi 10/10 (gold standard).

### Figure 3 — Short-read ↔ HiFi concordance
![Figure 3](figures/Figure3_concordance.png)

Per-species identity of the ngs45 (Illumina) unit to the easy45 (HiFi) consensus
on modern input — all successful species fall at 99.75–100 %, several at 0
mismatches.

### Figure 4 — Ribotype heterozygosity lowers consensus identity
![Figure 4](figures/Figure4_ribotype.png)

Identity to HiFi vs `ribotype_sites` (intragenomic heterozygosity). Homogeneous
arrays (0–6 sites) give a 0-mismatch consensus; heterozygous arrays (Citrus 29,
Oryza 15, Vitis 13) give a blended consensus at lower identity — a biological
signal (possible hybrid/allopolyploidy), not an assembly error.
